Fatigue

Understanding persistent tiredness and strategies to regain energy

What Is Fatigue?

Fatigue is more than just feeling tired - it's a persistent state of physical and/or mental exhaustion that doesn't improve with rest. It can affect your ability to function normally and significantly impact your quality of life, making even simple tasks feel overwhelming.

Common Causes

- Poor sleep quality or insufficient sleep
- Stress and emotional exhaustion
- Anemia or nutritional deficiencies
- Thyroid disorders
- Chronic illnesses (diabetes, heart disease, autoimmune conditions)
- Depression and anxiety
- Dehydration
- Sedentary lifestyle
- Medications side effects

Types of Fatigue

- Physical fatigue: Difficulty performing physical tasks
- Mental fatigue: Trouble concentrating, brain fog
- Emotional fatigue: Feeling drained emotionally
- Chronic fatigue: Persistent for 6+ months
- Adrenal fatigue: Related to stress hormone imbalance
- Post-viral fatigue: Following illness or infection

Energy-Boosting Strategies

- Prioritize 7-9 hours of quality sleep
- Stay hydrated and eat balanced meals
- Exercise regularly (even light activity helps)
- Practice stress management techniques
- Take short breaks throughout the day
- Limit caffeine and alcohol
- Establish consistent daily routines
- Address nutritional deficiencies

When to Seek Medical Advice

Consult a healthcare provider if fatigue persists for more than two weeks, is severe, interferes with daily activities, or is accompanied by symptoms like unexplained weight loss, fever, night sweats, or persistent pain. These could indicate underlying medical conditions that require proper diagnosis and treatment.
