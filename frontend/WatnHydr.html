Water & Hydration

Learn why hydration is essential, how water supports bodily functions, and signs of dehydration.

The Importance of Water

Water makes up around 60% of the human body. It plays a critical role in regulating body temperature, transporting nutrients, removing waste, lubricating joints, and supporting cellular functions.

Functions of Water in the Body

Water acts as a solvent, medium for biochemical reactions, and a transporter. It helps maintain blood volume and pressure, supports digestion, and keeps mucosal membranes moist for proper function.

Hydration Recommendations

Daily water needs vary based on age, activity level, climate, and diet. A common recommendation is about 2.7 liters for women and 3.7 liters for men per day, including fluid from food.

Signs of Dehydration

Common symptoms of dehydration include thirst, dry mouth, dark urine, fatigue, dizziness, and headache. Severe dehydration can lead to confusion, rapid heartbeat, and low blood pressure.

Hydration and Performance

Even mild dehydration can negatively impact cognitive performance, physical endurance, and thermoregulation. Athletes must pay close attention to fluid intake before, during, and after exercise.

Electrolytes and Fluid Balance

Electrolytes like sodium, potassium, and chloride help regulate water balance. Losses through sweat or illness must be replenished to maintain cellular function and hydration status.
