Essential Vitamins

Explore their functions, sources, and impact on your health

What Are Vitamins?

Vitamins are organic compounds required in small quantities for essential metabolic processes. Unlike macronutrients, they don't provide energy, but they're vital for health and wellbeing.

Fat-Soluble Vitamins (A, D, E, K)

These are stored in the body's fat tissues and liver. They play crucial roles in vision (Vitamin A), bone health and immunity (D), antioxidant defense (E), and blood clotting (K). Sources include fatty fish, dairy, leafy greens, and nuts. Overconsumption can lead to toxicity.

Water-Soluble Vitamins (B-complex, C)

These are not stored in the body and must be consumed regularly. B vitamins help with energy metabolism, while Vitamin C supports the immune system and collagen synthesis. Found in fruits, vegetables, grains, and lean proteins.

Common Deficiencies and Symptoms

- Vitamin D: Fatigue, bone pain, muscle weakness
- Vitamin B12: Anemia, numbness, memory issues
- Vitamin A: Night blindness
- Vitamin C: Bleeding gums, poor wound healing

Supplementation: When and Why

Supplements may be useful when dietary intake is inadequate or absorption is impaired. Always consult a healthcare provider before use. Natural food sources are usually better absorbed and safer.
