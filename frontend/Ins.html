Understanding Insulin

The hormone that regulates blood sugar, fat storage, and more

What is Insulin?

Insulin is a hormone produced by the pancreas that allows your cells to absorb glucose from the bloodstream. It's essential for maintaining stable blood sugar levels and providing energy to cells.

How It Works

After eating, blood glucose rises. In response, insulin is released to help shuttle glucose into cells. This reduces blood sugar levels and allows the body to use or store energy.

Insulin and Fat Storage

When insulin is elevated, fat breakdown is suppressed. The body stores more energy in fat cells, which is why chronic insulin spikes (e.g., from excessive sugar intake) may contribute to fat gain.

Foods and Insulin Response

Different foods trigger different insulin responses. Simple carbohydrates cause a rapid insulin spike, while protein and fiber-rich foods lead to a slower, more controlled release.

Insulin Sensitivity vs. Resistance

High insulin sensitivity means your body responds well to insulin, keeping blood sugar balanced. Resistance develops when cells stop responding, leading to elevated blood sugar and risk of type 2 diabetes.
