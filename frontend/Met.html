Understanding Metabolism

What it is, what affects it, and how you can influence it

What is Metabolism?

Metabolism refers to all chemical reactions in your body that convert food into energy. This energy is used for everything from breathing and circulating blood to physical activity and cellular repair.

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)

BMR is the number of calories your body needs at rest to maintain vital functions. It makes up the largest portion of your daily calorie burn.

What Influences Metabolism?

Several factors affect metabolic rate: age, muscle mass, genetics, hormone levels, and activity. Muscle is more metabolically active than fat, meaning it burns more energy even at rest.

Nutrition's Role

Protein-rich diets increase the thermic effect of food (TEF), meaning more calories are burned during digestion. Adequate nutrients also support thyroid and cellular functions that drive metabolism.

Activity and Exercise

Physical activity boosts metabolism both during and after workouts. Resistance training increases muscle mass, further raising resting energy expenditure.
