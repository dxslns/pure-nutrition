Calories and Energy

Understand how energy balance works, what calories represent, and how they relate to weight and performance

What Are Calories?

A calorie is a unit of energy. In nutrition, calories refer to the energy people get from the food and drink they consume, and the energy they use in physical activity. Understanding calories is key to understanding energy balance.

Energy Balance

Energy balance occurs when the number of calories consumed equals the number of calories the body uses. A positive energy balance (more in than out) leads to weight gain, while a negative energy balance (more out than in) leads to weight loss.

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)

BMR is the number of calories your body needs at rest to maintain vital functions such as breathing, circulation, and cell production. It accounts for the majority of your daily calorie expenditure.

Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE)

TDEE is the total number of calories you burn each day and includes BMR, physical activity, and the thermic effect of food (energy used for digestion). Adjusting your intake based on your TDEE helps you manage your weight.

Calories and Performance

Sufficient calorie intake is essential for optimal physical and mental performance. Too few calories can lead to fatigue, decreased concentration, and loss of muscle mass, while the right amount supports training, recovery, and mental clarity.

Energy Sources in Food

Each macronutrient provides a specific amount of energy: carbohydrates and proteins each provide 4 kcal per gram, while fats provide 9 kcal per gram. Alcohol also provides energy at 7 kcal per gram but is not considered essential.
